### 4. Communication

Communication in tech companies is extremely key. As often you'll be working on `6-12` person squads, which is just a tiny fraction of your product. Or even in startups you'll be interacting within your development team and stakeholders.

Without communication, you won't be to make it very far and if you're bad enough potentially find yourself without a job.

Nowadays to limit the risk of the above happening companies have started to introduce behavioural interviews/rounds of interviews. To help them filter out those who cannot work within a team or speak for themselves.

This is why in the `Behavioural/` directory we'll be focusing on the `STAR` method as well as going through how to link your answers to projects/experience you've worked on. There will also be examples of questions you might expect to be asked.

### 5. Dealing with Rejection

When applying for tech jobs you should learn to expect to be rejected and not to think every job you apply for will lead to a job.

The best advice I can give you is that when you apply to a company have the mindset of `I've done my best and no matter the outcome it'll be a great learning opportunity`. Don't go in cocky or over confident as when (`not if when`) you get rejected, it'll be heart crushing.
