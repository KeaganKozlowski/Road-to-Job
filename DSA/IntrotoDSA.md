# Introduction to DSA(Data Structures and Algorithms)

## Why do I need to know DSA?

If Leetcode is the test, then DSA is the textbook. You cannot pass an exam without knowledge of that topic. Unless you are really lucky, but in this job market with how competitive it is, it would be naive and idiotic to rely on luck. You need more than that, you need knowledge and understanding.

While grinding Leetcode is great for preparing for interviews, DSA is foundational. Because if you can't explain why you used a Linked list over an Array, interviewers will presume you guessed.

And they won't want someone careless like that at the company, you'll likely be rejected. As the risk of hiring you could lead to Data breaches, fines or worse.

### DSA vs Leetcode

Learning DSA is about understanding your tools and why we used them over others (like when to use a hammer or a screwdriver). Leetcode is about being thrown into someone's back garden and told to build a tree house in 2 hours with your tools and knowledge.

**Do not jump into Leetcodes without at least foundational knowledge of DSA**

## How will this work?

Each Data Structure and Algorithm will have its own folder containing:
- Explanation with a breakdown, how to recognize it, and its Time/Space complexity
- A pseudocode implementation with comments
- And potentially a diagram based on its complexity

## My advice to understanding and mastering DSA

1. Understand how exactly something works, don't be afraid to research into detail. Like for example with Python's `Dict()`, don't just assume it works. You should understand it's a Hash Map, how hashing functions work, and what collision is.
2. Code it from scratch once: Using the pseudocode provided use that to write your own implementation or if you're in for a challenge try your attempt first only using the pseudocode when stuck.
3. Understand `Big-O-Notation` as when `N` is small the complexity doesn't matter, but as the complexity increases it becomes more and more important.
4. Focus on identifying patterns, there are 1000s of algorithms but only about 15-20 core patterns needed for interviews.
